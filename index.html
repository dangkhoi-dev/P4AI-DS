8. Key Insights & Conclusion

  • Demographic Disparity: Gender and Age were the strongest demographic predictors of survival. Females exhibited a drastically higher survival rate compared to males, reflecting the "women and children first" protocol.
  • Socio-Economic Impact: Pclass and Fare strongly correlated with survival length. First-class passengers had a significantly higher survival rate than those in third class.
  • Distribution Shapes & Outliers: Features like Fare and SibSp are immensely right-skewed with heavy kurtosis. These massive outliers require logarithmic transformations before modeling.

Key Insights: Dataset Overview

  • No missing data is found in the main text column, ensuring a clean pipeline.

2. Distribution & Length Analysis

Fig 2.1: High class imbalance is observed, with ~86.6% of messages labeled as 'Ham'.
Fig 2.2: Spam messages are significantly longer on average and cluster sharply around 150-160 characters.
Fig 2.3: Scatter plot mapping character count against word count, partitioned by label.

Key Insights: Structure & Labels

  • Class Imbalance: The target distribution is highly imbalanced (~86.6% Ham). Evaluation should use F1-Score instead of Accuracy.
  • Message Length: Spam messages are significantly longer on average. Length itself acts as a strong predictive feature.

3. Vocabulary & Bigram Analysis

Fig 3.1: Most frequent words in the dataset before any filtering.
Fig 3.2: High frequency of non-informative stopwords that require removal.
Fig 3.3: Most frequent words after English stopwords are removed.
Fig 3.4: Bigrams effectively capture Spam-specific phrases like 'customer service' or 'free entry'.
Fig 3.5: Visual representation of vocabulary dominance in Ham (Green) vs Spam (Red).

Key Insights: N-Gram Context

  • Spam Signatures: Promotional phrases like "free entry", "claim your", and "txt" dominate the spam vocabulary.
  • Ham Signatures: Normal messages are dominated by conversational fragments like "are you", "i am", "let me".
  • Action Taken: Using TF-IDF with Bigrams will provide excellent discriminative features for classification.

Key Insights: Image Overview

  • Perfect Balance: The dataset is perfectly balanced with exactly 5,000 images per class. No oversampling techniques are required.
  • Resolution: The uniform 32x32 pixel size eliminates the need for complex resizing preprocessing.

2. Image Statistics & RGB Distribution

Fig 2.1: Perfectly balanced dataset distribution across all 10 categories.
Fig 2.2: Histogram visualizing the distribution of pixel intensities across the Red, Green, and Blue channels.

Key Insights: Color & Pixel Analysis

  • Color Tone Bias: The Blue channel has the lowest mean intensity (~113.8), indicating an overall warmer tone across the dataset.
  • Variance: A high standard deviation (~62-66) across all channels reflects significant diversity in brightness and contrast.
  • Recommendation: Apply pixel normalization (scaling to [0,1]) to stabilize feature extraction during CNN training.
